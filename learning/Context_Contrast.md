> The context is the part of a passage in which a particular word is used and which helps to explain that word.

* It is the responsibility of every driver to be entirely sover at all times. Drunk Drivers pose a danger to themselves, to their passengers, and to everyone else on the road.<br>
  → Clue: Drunk Target: Sober
* One sandwitch for lunch usually suffices for you, but for me it is not enough.<br>
  → Clue: Enough Target: Suffice

> Sometimes tense/parts of speech for contrasting words may not necessarily match;

* Don't digress and stick to the topic.
* Out tennis coach will neither confirm nor deny the rumor that she is going to be the basketball coach next year.
  *** neither A nor B
* Your account of the fight concurs with Joanne's but differs from the accounts given by the other witnesses.<br>
→ Clue: differ Target: concur
* Roses in bloom are a common sight in summer, but a rarity in late Nov.
* The tables in the restaurant **were all occupied**, and we waited more than ten minutes for one to **become vacant.**
* There are few theaters here, but on Broadway there are theaters galore.
  
> Sometimes time modal of contrasting words may not match too.

* I do not shrink from this responsibility; I welcome it.
* Ruth is an experienced driver, but Harry is a novice; he began taking lessons last month.

  → Clue: genuine, Target: counterfeit

→ Clue: friendly and supportive Target: hostile

## Ⅰ. 직접적 대비 (기초)

1. A bank teller can usually tell the difference between **genuine** $100 bills and **counterfeit** ones.<br>  
→ Clue: genuine / Target: counterfeit
    
2. The room was not **silent** but filled with a constant **hum**.<br>  
    → Clue: silent / Target: hum
    
3. She expected the task to be **easy**, but it turned out to be quite **difficult**.<br>  
    → Clue: easy / Target: difficult
    
4. His explanation was **clear**, not **confusing**.<br>  
    → Clue: clear / Target: confusing
    
5. The water felt **warm** rather than **cold**.<br>  
    → Clue: warm / Target: cold
    
6. This path is **safe**, unlike the **dangerous** shortcut.<br>  
    → Clue: safe / Target: dangerous
    
7. The manager praised her **honesty** and criticized his **deceit**.<br>  
    → Clue: honesty / Target: deceit
    
8. The movie was **funny**, not **tragic**.<br>  
    → Clue: funny / Target: tragic
    
9. He chose to **accept** the offer instead of **rejecting** it.<br>  
    → Clue: accept / Target: reject
    
10. The instructions were **simple**, not **complex**.<br>  
    → Clue: simple / Target: complex
    
11. The road ahead looked **bright**, not **dark**.<br>  
    → Clue: bright / Target: dark
    
12. Her answer was **correct**, whereas mine was **wrong**.<br>  
    → Clue: correct / Target: wrong
    
13. The patient felt **better**, not **worse**, after the treatment.<br>  
    → Clue: better / Target: worse
    
14. He spoke in a **calm** voice, never **angry**.<br>  
    → Clue: calm / Target: angry
    
15. The test was **fair**, not **biased**.<br>  
    → Clue: fair / Target: biased
